Interesting Facts about Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels

Interesting Facts about Cicads

  • Cicadas go through several stages of development, called instars.
  • Cicadas in different regions of the eastern United States often sound slightly different, which is to be expected given that they are separated geographically.
  • However, these differences are more pronounced in areas where two species overlap.

Interesting Facts about Teddy

  • A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  • Commercially made, mass-produced teddy bears are predominantly made as toys for children.
  • Developed apparently simultaneously by toymakers Morris Michtom in the U.S. and Richard Steiff in Germany in the early years of the 20th century, and named after President Theodore "Teddy" Roosevelt, the teddy bear became a popular children's toy, celebrated in story, song, and film.
